# Motor Imagery Session Guide (Participant Instructions)

This session collects EEG during **left vs right motor imagery**.
You will not physically move your hands during the task.

## Goal
When cued, imagine trying to **lift one hand at the wrist** as if it is lightly restrained against the desk.

- `LEFT` cue: imagine effort only in the **left** wrist/hand.
- `RIGHT` cue: imagine effort only in the **right** wrist/hand.

Keep this imagery style the same across all trials.

## Seating and Posture Setup
Please set up exactly this way before starting:

1. Sit upright in the chair with your back supported.
2. Keep both feet flat on the floor.
3. Rest your elbows comfortably on the arm rests.
4. Place both forearms forward so hands are over the desk.
5. Keep both hands **palm down** on the desk surface.
6. Keep shoulders relaxed and level (no leaning/twisting).
7. Keep head centered and still.
8. Look at the center fixation cross on the screen.

Posture should be **symmetrical** and stable for the whole run.

## What to Do During Each Trial
Each trial has three parts:

1. **Fixation / pause**: stay still and relaxed.
2. **Prepare (arrow shown)**: get ready for the cued side (left or right).
3. **Perform imagery**: do motor imagery for the cued side for the full period.

During **Perform imagery**:

- Imagine trying to lift only the cued hand at the wrist.
- Keep the hand physically still, as if restrained by the desk.
- Feel effort in wrist/forearm (kinesthetic feeling), not just visual imagination.
- Keep the opposite hand completely relaxed.
- Continue the same imagined effort until the cue ends.

## Clean Data Rules (Very Important)
To keep the EEG usable and consistent:

1. **Do not physically move** hands, fingers, wrists, shoulders, or trunk.
2. **Minimize eye blinks during Perform imagery** windows.
3. If you must blink, do so during fixation/ITI periods.
4. Keep jaw relaxed; avoid clenching teeth or moving tongue.
5. Avoid facial movements (frowning, eyebrow raising, talking).
6. Keep breathing calm and regular (no breath-holding).
7. Stay focused on the fixation cross.
8. Do not count out loud or subvocalize words.

## Consistency Tips

- Use the same internal phrase each trial (example: "left lift" / "right lift"), silently.
- Keep imagery intensity moderate and stable; avoid bursts.
- Do not switch strategy mid-session.
- If attention drifts, reset at next fixation and continue.

## Breaks

- Use break screens to blink, swallow, and relax.
- Re-establish the same posture before resuming.

## If You Make a Mistake
If you accidentally move, blink repeatedly during imagery, or lose focus:

- Stay calm and continue.
- Re-center at the next fixation period.
- Resume the same imagery strategy.

## Quick Checklist Before Starting

- [ ] Sitting upright, comfortable, symmetric posture
- [ ] Elbows supported on arm rests
- [ ] Forearms forward, hands palm down on desk
- [ ] Shoulders, jaw, and face relaxed
- [ ] Understand: LEFT cue = left wrist lift imagery only; RIGHT cue = right only
- [ ] Ready to minimize blinks and movement during Perform imagery

Thank you. Consistent imagery and stillness are the most important factors for high-quality motor imagery EEG data.
